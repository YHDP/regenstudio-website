Energy Hubs: How We Design Them as Smart Energy Communities

Building smart energy systems that empower local communities to take control of their energy futures.

Yvo Hunink de Paiva Regen Studio
18 September 2024
5 min read

Smart energy systems are more than technology. They are carried by local communities realising they can take power back over their own energy grids. Here's how we design Energy Hubs as smart energy communities.

At Regen Studio, our expertise lies in designing smart energy systems, which optimize the generation, distribution, and consumption of energy. These systems are designed to improve energy efficiency, support the integration of renewable resources, reduce emissions and prevent grid congestion issues. This is more than just a technical approach. Such systems are carried by local communities, that in this new energy era are realizing they can, and sometimes need, to take power back over their own energy grids. So what we are really building are smart energy communities, or nowadays often referred to as Energy Hubs.

The Basics of Smart Energy Systems

Smart energy systems represent a new approach to energy management. They integrate various sources of energy — such as solar and wind — with innovative storage solutions, creating a dynamic system that balances supply and demand in real-time. This intelligent management reduces inefficiencies, mitigates grid congestion, and maximizes the use of renewable energy.

Our systems are based on digital energy management technologies, which allow for predictive control and optimization. By utilizing data-driven decision-making, we ensure that energy is distributed efficiently and only when and where it is needed, reducing overall waste. Such a system contains the following key components:

  • Decentralized Energy Generation: Decentralized sources of renewable energy, such as rooftop solar panels, are essential for the energy transition. By producing energy locally, we reduce the need for long-distance energy transport, but with enough scale it also brings new problems that we need to solve to complete the transition.
  • Flexible Energy Assets: Smart energy systems require not just efficient storage to handle the intermittent nature of renewable energy and ensure that excess energy generated during peak periods can be stored for use during times of high demand. Any flexibly controlled asset can participate in managing energy flows, such as heat pumps or flexible data center server activity.
  • Real-Time Data Structure: If you don't measure, you don't know. And the higher the detail we can get, the better, sometimes less than a second. This requires smart meters in each point in the network that have robust connectivity and data endpoints to a common place where the data is stored that is accessible to the users.
  • Predictive Analytics: With the use of real-time data to monitor energy flows, real time predictions are also possible. With advanced analytics, we can predict consumption patterns, market dynamics, weather patterns and even human activities such as events in the public space.
  • Local Grid Management: With the help of predictions, action can be taken. To prevent grid overload, demand-response technologies are designed with the community preferences embedded. This enables businesses and communities to adjust their energy usage dynamically, helping balance the grid while adhering to their own priorities.
  • Governance Model: For effective management of a local energy grid, a collaboration needs to be structured between the parties. This can take several shapes, such as a coöperative, a business entity, or a foundation. This is where the community really kicks in to making collective decisions.

How We Design Energy Hubs as Smart Energy Communities

At Regen Studio, we approach smart energy systems and hubs as a community first. With a focus on building robust, resilient systems that empower local stakeholders to take control of their energy futures. Our projects emphasize community governance and collaboration, ensuring that the benefits of energy independence are distributed equitably among all participants. Communities can reduce carbon footprints together more effectively, lower energy costs, and contribute to regional energy security. The key in getting this right, is a very thorough analysis of the differentiated needs of each stakeholder. This community exercise will have a profound impact on the design of your technical system, and cannot be ignored. When all such elemental things are decided, it is time to formalize the legal structure, often by using a constitution that supports the agreements of the stakeholders. There is a lot of nitty-gritty detail, that fortunately we are figuring out how to structure.

Regen Studio's Expertise: Living Lab Scheveningen

Regen Studio has extensive experience working on cutting-edge energy projects, including our involvement in the Living Lab Scheveningen with the City of The Hague. This initiative, which explores the potential of smart grid systems in the harbour area at the beach, aims to create a sustainable local energy ecosystem by integrating renewable energy, energy storage, and intelligent energy management. The project showcases how decentralized energy generation, local storage, and a flexible grid can work together to improve energy security and sustainability in urban environments.

Regen Studio has helped the project design the community, onboard the distribution company, sketch the required technical system, form the project proposals, designed the legal structure of the coöperative, filed the funding requests and started the procurement of the open-source energy management system.

Through our work in Scheveningen, we have helped demonstrate how smart grids can operate in complex, real-world environments. Our experience in this project has strengthened our expertise in designing scalable, future-proof energy systems that can be adapted to meet the unique needs of any community or business.

Shaping the Future of Energy Management

Whether we are working on a small neighborhood energy project or a large industrial site, our approach remains the same: to create efficient, flexible, and sustainable energy systems that support local communities and contribute to broader environmental goals. With each project, we aim to push the boundaries of what's possible in energy management, ensuring that our solutions are not only sustainable but also just and ready for the challenges of tomorrow.

As the world continues to shift toward renewable energy, smart energy systems will play an increasingly critical role in ensuring energy security and sustainability. Regen Studio is at the forefront of this transformation, building the future of energy, today.
