Europe’s activities surrounding the Ecodesign for Sustainable Products Regulation (ESPR) are ramping up and will impact many products. The ESPR Working Plan 2025–2030 is the new operating system for that reality. It sets the order and tempo for ecodesign requirements for specific product groups and the obligation to report on those through Digital Product Passports (DPPs).

What Product Groups are Impacted by the ESPR Working Plan?

Apart from batteries — which were already adopted — 22 more product groups have been defined, each set to receive a ‘Delegated Act’ containing specifics on data, governance, and technical requirements.
